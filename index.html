About
How I approach product

I start from the customer and work backward. My job is turning ambiguous AI capability into products that people actually use and pay for, and the only reliable way I know to get there is to obsess over the real user problem before writing a line of strategy.

From there I let data and clear goals do the steering. A/B tests with statistical rigor, evals, and customer research decide the roadmap, and every initiative ties back to a measurable outcome rather than activity. I care as much about unit economics and token cost as I do about the experience, because an AI product only matters if it scales sustainably.

I also work in an AI-native way. I build with Claude Code and Cursor to synthesize customer feedback, analyze data, write PRDs, and prototype, which has multiplied my product velocity several times over without trading away quality.

ElasticSenior PM, Product Lead, 2025 to Present

Agent Builder, an agentic conversational AI platform

Owning vision, strategy, and roadmap end to end for a new agentic platform.

Context

Elastic needed a first-party way for developers to build and run AI agents on top of its search and data platform. I joined as product lead for Agent Builder, a net-new agentic conversational AI platform, and owned vision, strategy, and roadmap from a blank page.

Problem

Developers wanted to build agents over their own data but were stitching together brittle tooling. The bet was that a unified agentic experience native to Elastic could win the developer, but only if it shipped fast, monetized, and stayed cost-efficient at scale.

What I did and the tradeoffs

I scoped aggressively to get a credible product to GA in three months, then prioritized monetization over breadth by building the pricing and packaging for hosted billing and pay-as-you-go models before chasing every feature. To scale sustainably, I partnered with engineering and data science on context-enrichment and context-engineering strategies that cut token cost rather than simply adding capability. I deliberately chose a product-led-growth motion, embedding Agent Builder into the developer ecosystem through partnerships with Google, Anthropic, Cursor, and Microsoft, over a top-down sales push. I also aligned more than seven cross-functional teams to extend it across Elastic's offerings into one unified experience instead of fragmented per-product agents.

What I would carry forward

Shipping monetization early, by month five, forced clarity on who the product was really for. The biggest unlock was not a feature. It was the decision to unify into one agentic experience across teams, which turned seven or more roadmaps into a single growth curve.

IntuitStaff PM, 2021 to 2025

Intuit Assist, a GenAI financial assistant at global scale

Zero-to-one GenAI growth and support strategy for Intuit's global financial assistant.

Context

I pioneered the zero-to-one GenAI growth and support strategy for Intuit Assist, Intuit's Financial Assistant, serving more than 100 million customers worldwide. I led a team of more than 40 people from ideation through a launch that the CEO unveiled at the Investor Summit, then scaled it across three business units.

Problem

Customers needed trustworthy, in-product help across complex financial and tax tasks. Generic LLM answers were not safe or accurate enough, so the assistant had to contain support demand, raise satisfaction, and work across web, mobile, text, and voice without hallucinating in a high-stakes domain.

What I did and the tradeoffs

I treated response quality as the product rather than coverage. I partnered with data science to enrich models with tax and accounting knowledge through RAG and Graph-RAG retrieval, ranking, and attribution, and I refined NLP intents and tone sensitivity with AI safety features such as proactive profanity filtering and bias detection, accepting a slower rollout in exchange for trust. I automated in-product routing and ticket creation to lift service efficiency, drove the experience through rigorous A/B/C experimentation rather than intuition, and made the deliberate call to scale across surfaces and business units only after the core experience had proven out.

In a high-stakes domain, safety and quality are the growth strategy. The RAG and Graph-RAG knowledge enrichment that felt unglamorous was what made the assistant trustworthy enough to scale, and that trust is what moved resolution, containment, and CSAT.

IntuitStaff PM, 2021 to 2025

ML-powered recommendation and personalization engine

Zero-to-one recommendation engine driving retention, cross-sell, and personalized growth.

Context

Alongside Intuit Assist, I built a zero-to-one ML-driven recommendation engine for cross-sell and content across churning B2C and B2B customers, plus the personalization and experimentation systems behind it, across US and international markets.

Problem

Retained customers were under-monetized and receiving generic experiences, and product teams lacked a rigorous way to test what actually moved revenue. The opportunity was to use personalization and disciplined experimentation to surface the right next product or action without degrading the experience with irrelevant prompts.

What I did and the tradeoffs

I prioritized ranking and retrieval quality and built a Data Enrichment platform capability to improve personalized targeting and segmentation, gating cross-sell on relevance signals rather than maximizing surface area. I partnered across design, research, legal, compliance, marketing, engineering, and data science to develop and test hyper-personalized experiences on web, mobile, text, and voice, and I designed rigorous A/B tests with statistical rigor to translate complex findings into product improvements. I also simplified the in-product search experience with AI, and built early AI testing frameworks to evaluate model performance and surface bias before scaling.

What I would carry forward

Personalization wins are won on relevance, not volume. Building the data-enrichment and bias-testing foundations early let us scale recommendations and targeting confidently instead of eroding trust for short-term lift.

NetAppPM, Enterprise e-Commerce, 2017 to 2021

Zero-to-one marketplace and AI chatbot for enterprise commerce

Building a digital marketplace and AI support from scratch within an enterprise sales motion.

Context

I owned digital commerce and AI-powered marketplace products at NetApp, building a zero-to-one freemium trial marketplace for SaaS and hardware, spanning NetApp and partner products from AWS, GCP, Azure, HP, Hitachi, and Dell-EMC, plus an AI chatbot, inside a traditionally sales-led enterprise organization.

Problem

Prospects had no low-friction way to evaluate products, and support could not scale to the growing volume of interactions. I needed to prove that a self-serve, AI-assisted motion could generate real pipeline in an enterprise context, and to quantify the revenue gap in order to win executive buy-in.

What I did and the tradeoffs

I quantified revenue gaps worth $380M and took business plans to the C-suite to fund the bet. I built the freemium trial marketplace from scratch and integrated it with Salesforce CRM and marketing automation, generating $35M+ in pipeline so that self-serve fed the existing sales motion rather than competing with it. I shipped a zero-to-one AI chatbot and optimized its NLP pipelines for operational scale, reaching 10,000 daily interactions at 95% intent accuracy, choosing reliability and accuracy over breadth. I then iterated rapidly with design and engineering to add order tracking, reminders, notifications, and automated re-order placement, and led a global team on a go-to-market strategy that lifted subscription renewals.

In an enterprise sales culture, a self-serve product wins by feeding the sales motion rather than fighting it. Wiring the marketplace into CRM is what turned evaluations into a $500M opportunity pipeline.
